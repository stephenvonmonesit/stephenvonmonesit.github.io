02 — A Final Reflection

Maybe the better question isn't "why do women choose red flag men?" — it's why we made unhealthy behavior look attractive in the first place.

01

Not every confident man has good character — and not every quiet man lacks strength.

02

Not every "nice guy" is genuinely kind, and not every "bad boy" is beyond change.

03

Real love isn't found in emotional games, constant anxiety, or hoping someone becomes a different person.

04

The healthiest relationships are usually the least dramatic — built on trust, respect, consistency, and peace.

These patterns describe increased risk, not destiny. Attachment style, trauma, low self-esteem, and culture can all raise the odds of choosing an unhealthy partner — but none of them make it inevitable.

Because at the end of the day, the biggest green flag isn't someone who makes your heart race — it's someone who makes your heart feel safe.

A gentle reminder

Healing does not require perfect hindsight. It begins with noticing the pattern, naming it, and choosing a steadier way forward.
